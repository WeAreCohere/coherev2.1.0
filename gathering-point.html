the gathering point

Gathering Points are regular online and in-person spaces hosted by refugee-led organisations, where displaced communities and allies come together around key themes and crises. Grounded in lived experience, they foster regional exchange, multilingual dialogue, and grassroots leadership rooted in solidarity and action.

Why Join a Gathering Point?

Gathering Points are welcoming spaces—both online and in-person—designed for displaced communities, refugee-led organisations, and allies to come together. By joining, you’ll be part of regular sessions that focus on urgent issues, thematic areas, and shared challenges.

These gatherings centre the lived experience of displacement, offering space to listen, share stories, and co-create practical solutions. They foster multilingual, regional exchange and amplify grassroots leadership—building solidarity across borders.

Whether you’re looking to connect with others, elevate your voice, or contribute to collective action, Gathering Points are a powerful place to start. Join us to be part of something grounded in empathy, driven by community, and built for change.

Gathering Point for congo

The Gathering Point for Congo is a dynamic platform where refugee-led organizations and community leaders converge to exchange insights, spotlight urgent issues, and co-create solutions. Whether hosted in local hubs or across digital networks, each event prioritizes grassroots experience, multilingual dialogue, and collective action across regions.

Each session centers the lived experiences of grassroots leaders, creating opportunities for:

Storytelling

Voices from those at the forefront of community action.

Multilingual Dialogue

Interpretation in Swahili, French, and English enables inclusive engagement.

Thematic Breakouts

Focused rooms that foster active participation and solidarity.

Cross-Regional Understanding

Connecting local responses with global community support.

Why the Focus on Congo

The Democratic Republic of Congo has long faced overlapping crises—armed conflict, displacement, gender-based violence, and under-resourced healthcare systems. But within these challenges exists deep resilience, led by communities who continue to advocate, rebuild, and respond.

  • Refugee-led organizations are already driving grassroots change across the region
  • Eastern Congo hosts thousands of displaced persons from Rwanda, Burundi, and beyond
  • Women and children in conflict zones face urgent risks to health and safety
  • Local leadership deserves global attention—beyond headlines, through support
